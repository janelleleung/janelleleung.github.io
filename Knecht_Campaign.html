Knecht Campaign

Explore the website @ Kathy Knecht

Overview:

I joined the AZ LD21 Knecht Campaign under the Bluebonnet Fellowship, an organization the works to connect campaigns with volunteers with experience in tech. During my time here, I helped revamp the campaign’s Facebook page, managed and analyzed campaign data, created content, and edited videos. With my previous experience of managing JCL Immigration’s digital identity and previous experience competing in speech competitions about modern American topics, I was able to create customized content based on the Knecht Campaign’s goals.

Although Kathy Knecht was running as a Democrat, you will find an absence of red and blue and partisan related words, because the foundation of what Kathy Knecht is running on is being a candidate for all people and to set aside bipartisan conflict. Thus, the Knecht uses color and text to affirm the candidate’s values and goals.





Build:

Role: Bluebonnet Fellow (Content Creator, Editor, Data Analyzer)
Tools: ActionSprout, Canva, Illustrator, Photoshop, Outvote, Google Sheets, VAN Data, Deck, BallotReady

Brand Goals:

Trust & Representation
Kathy Knecht’s Campaign is running to represent all people, regardless of political party and voter status. It is essential that volunteers and voter understand this and phrases such as “Flip the house” and “Go Blue” are avoided because this assumes that the other side is losing instead of gaining something.

Experience
Voters are likely to trust a candidate the more experience that candidate has. Thus, it was important to highlight Kathy Knecht’s past leadership, volunteer, and government experience to reflect that Knecht is knowledgeable and capable.

Overall Design and Accomplishments:

• Inclusive and welcoming images to encourage all voters and volunteers
• Strong and bolded words to highlight strengths of candidate
