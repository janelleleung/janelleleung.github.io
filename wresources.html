Wellesley Resources App

Learn more about the school @ Wellesley College




Overview:

From June-July 2020, I worked with a team at Wellesley College to design and create a website for a Wellesley College Resources App. The objective of this app is to provide a way to make campus resources more easily available and known for students that may need them throughout the year, especially as students return to the fall during COVID-19.






Build:

Role: UI/UX Designer
Tools: Illustrator, Balsamiq Cloud, InVision, User Testing, Bootstrap, HTML, CSS, JavaScript
Timeline: June 2020 – July 2020 (future iterations currently in work)




Problem Analysis:

There is a plethora of resources for campus life available to Wellesley College students, but many students are unaware of their existence or unsure of how to access them. To combat the confusion surround resources at Wellesley College, we have created a app specifically dedicated to help Wellesley students know where resources are, what resources are available, when resources are available, and which resources can help them. From contacting residential assistants to campus police, Wellesley Resources is there 24/7 to help students out.

“…Wellesley’s resources are a magnet not only for the surrounding community and metropolitan area; they attract attention—and scholars—from around the world.” – Wellesley College



Research:

Users’ demands
Potential users can find resources quickly and efficiently by navigating the app. Wellesley Resources App users can navigate the app in various ways to find help according to their own preference.

Important features after analysis
• Consistent design parallel with Wellesley College style guidelines
• Buttons that lead into subsections
• Essential information provided on resources listed including phone number, location, head contact, and other notes
• Easy to access additional information
• Simplicity and organization


Personas:

Typical Wellesley Student
• Hopes to live a balanced life (exercise, sleep, academics, and social life)
• Aspires to attend graduate schools
• Experiences anxiety with heavy course load
• Feels as there is not enough time to do everything
• Frustrated because people next to her in her dorm are always playing loud music

Mental Health Focused Wellesley Student
• Strives to succeed in classes and graduate on time
• Seeks medication and therapy for mental health issues
• Faces confusions using the appointment portal for the Stone Health Center
• Struggles with anxiety and depression since she was a teenager
• Mental health issues cause her to miss work shifts and submit work late or incomplete
